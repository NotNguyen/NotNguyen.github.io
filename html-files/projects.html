7th Grade Project
I made a battery using pennies.

Materials: 2 copper wires, a key chain light bulb, 10 pennies, vinegar, salt, paper towels, aluminum foil, cardboard paper, pen, alligator wire clips (optional- mainly to make it easier to connect to things)

Procedure:
  1. In a small bowl or glass, mix together 1/4 C. of vinegar (electrolyte) and 1 Tbsp./3 Tsp. of salt (ions). Stir well.
  2. Gather some pennies, wash with vinegar and salt. This is just a preliminary step to remove dirt and grime.
  3. Get a medium square of aluminum and fold it over and over again until you can put a penny on top of it.
  4. Use the pen and trace a penny on top the folded aluminum foil. Then cut the circle. You should end up with several foil circles. *Note: Make sure to peel apart the foil because they might be sticking to others.
  5. Use the pen and trace a penny on top of the cardboard paper Do this step over and over again until you have 10 circles. Then cut all of the circles out.
  6. Pour a some vinegar in a small bowl or glass, then put all of cardboard circles in the bowl or glass.
  7. Start building your stack:
    1. put down a penny on a napkin or plate.
    2. take one of your cardboard circle out of the bowl and place it on the penny. *Note: Make sure your cardboard circles aren't soaking wet or it might short-circuit your battery
    3. put one foil circle, that you cut out, on top of the cardboard circle. *Note: Make sure the foil's edges don't touch the other pieces of foil
    4. repeat all of step 7 until you run out of circles
  8. place down a piece of tape sticky side up and place one end of a wire on the tape. Then place your battery on top of your wire. Place the other wire on top of your battery.
  9. Wrap the tape around the battery and the wires.
  10. Attach the two wires or (alligator clips) to your light bulb. Tape them together (optional) to hold them together so you don't have to hold it.
  11. Your battery could last up to 3 days at most if you do it correctly
Hypothesis:
My battery that is made out of pennies, foil, cardboard paper, and vinegar. I think that my battery will last up to 3 days at most. It can only light a flash light bulb. I think that this battery is easy to make and soon a lot of people will build their own batteries. I think that the more pennies I put into my battery, my battery will be stronger and more long lasting.
Why it helps in the community: If someone was in need of a battery then they could just build one easily
Name of the project: Pennies for a battery
